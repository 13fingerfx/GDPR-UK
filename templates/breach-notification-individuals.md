# Personal Data Breach Notification to Individuals — Template (UK GDPR Art 34)

> Required **without undue delay** where the breach is likely to result in a **high risk** to individuals' rights and freedoms. Must be in **clear and plain language** (Art 34(2)) — no legalese, no minimising spin. Exceptions: data unintelligible to unauthorised persons (e.g. properly encrypted, keys uncompromised); subsequent measures have removed the high risk; or individual notice would involve disproportionate effort → use a public communication of equivalent effectiveness instead (Art 34(3)).
>
> Reputation management is not a lawful reason to delay or soften. Coordinate timing with law enforcement only where they advise notification would prejudice an investigation — document that advice.

---

Subject: **Important: a security incident affecting your personal data**

Dear {{name}},

We are writing to tell you about a data security incident at {{organisation}} that affects your personal information. We are sorry this has happened. This email explains what happened, what information was involved, what we are doing, and the steps you can take to protect yourself.

**What happened**

On {{date}}, {{plain-language description: e.g. "an attacker gained access to one of our customer databases" / "an employee's laptop containing customer records was stolen"}}. We discovered this on {{date}} and immediately {{containment step}}.

**What information was involved** [Art 34(2) → 33(3)(a)]

The information relating to you that was involved: {{specific categories — e.g. name, email address, hashed password, order history}}. {{If applicable}}: It did **not** include {{e.g. full card numbers, passwords in readable form}}.

**What this could mean for you** [33(3)(c)]

{{Honest, concrete statement of likely consequences — e.g. "You may receive phishing emails that use your name and refer to your account. There is a risk of attempted fraud using these details."}}

**What we are doing** [33(3)(d)]

{{Containment, investigation, remediation; ICO notified on {{date}}; law enforcement involved if so; support offered — e.g. credit monitoring for N months at our cost.}}

**What you can do**

- {{e.g. Change your password on our service — and anywhere you reused it}}
- {{e.g. Be alert to emails, calls or texts claiming to be from us; we will never ask for your full password}}
- {{e.g. Watch statements for unfamiliar transactions; contact your bank if concerned}}
- {{e.g. You can check advice at ncsc.gov.uk and report suspected fraud to Action Fraud (actionfraud.police.uk / 0300 123 2040)}}

**Contact us** [33(3)(b)]

Our {{Data Protection Officer / privacy team}} can answer your questions: {{email, phone, hours}}. {{If volume is expected: dedicated helpline details.}}

You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner ([ico.org.uk](https://ico.org.uk), 0303 123 1113) — though we would welcome the chance to help you first.

{{Signature — a named senior person, not "The Team"}}
