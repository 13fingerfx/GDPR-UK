# Data Processing Agreement — Template (UK GDPR Art 28)

> Controller ↔ processor agreement containing every Art 28(3) mandatory clause. Where processing leaves the UK, complete Annex C with the IDTA or UK Addendum (`sources/ico-idta.pdf`, `sources/ico-addendum.pdf`).

## 1. Definitions

Terms defined in the UK GDPR (personal data, processing, data subject, personal data breach, etc.) have those meanings. "Data Protection Law" means the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, each as amended (including by the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025), and PECR where applicable.

## 2. Details of processing [Art 28(3) chapeau]

As set out in **Annex A**: subject matter; duration; nature and purpose of processing; types of personal data; categories of data subjects; and the obligations and rights of the Controller.

## 3. Processor obligations

The Processor shall:

1. **Documented instructions** [28(3)(a)]: process the personal data only on the Controller's documented instructions (including as to transfers outside the UK), unless required otherwise by UK law — in which case the Processor shall inform the Controller before processing unless that law prohibits it.
2. **Confidentiality** [28(3)(b)]: ensure persons authorised to process the data are bound by contractual or statutory confidentiality obligations.
3. **Security** [28(3)(c), Art 32]: implement and maintain the technical and organisational measures in **Annex B**, reviewing them as risk evolves.
4. **Sub-processors** [28(2), 28(4)]: engage sub-processors only with the Controller's {{prior specific written authorisation / general written authorisation per the list in Annex D, with {{30}} days' notice of changes during which the Controller may object}}; impose the same data protection obligations by contract; and remain fully liable to the Controller for sub-processor performance.
5. **Data subject rights** [28(3)(e)]: taking into account the nature of the processing, assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organisational measures in responding to requests under UK GDPR Chapter III (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, Arts 22A–22D safeguards), and pass any request received directly to the Controller within {{2}} business days without responding to it save as instructed.
6. **Assistance** [28(3)(f)]: assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with Arts 32–36 (security, breach notification, DPIAs, prior consultation), taking into account the nature of the processing and information available.
7. **Breach notification** [33(2)]: notify the Controller **without undue delay** (and in any event within {{24/48}} hours) after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting the Controller's data, providing the information in Art 33(3) as it becomes available, and cooperate with the Controller's own Art 33/34 obligations. The Processor shall not notify the ICO or data subjects on the Controller's behalf unless instructed.
8. **Return or deletion** [28(3)(g)]: at the end of the provision of services, at the Controller's choice, delete or return all personal data and delete existing copies, unless UK law requires storage; deletion to be certified on request.
9. **Audit** [28(3)(h)]: make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with Art 28, and allow for and contribute to audits and inspections by the Controller or its mandated auditor, on {{reasonable notice, frequency cap, confidentiality conditions}}. The Processor shall **immediately inform the Controller if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes Data Protection Law**.

## 4. International transfers

The Processor shall not transfer personal data outside the UK except as documented in **Annex C** (destination, recipient, transfer mechanism — UK adequacy regulations, IDTA, or UK Addendum — and any supplementary measures) or with the Controller's prior written consent.
